CHANDRAYAN

"Chandrayaan-1" was India's first lunar probe. Launched on October 22, 2008, by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), Chandrayaan-1 aimed to study the Moon from orbit and gather data about its surface composition and mineralogy. The mission also aimed to search for water molecules on the lunar surface.

"Chandrayaan-2" is India's second lunar exploration mission, launched by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). It builds upon the success of the first Chandrayaan mission and is designed to further advance India's lunar exploration capabilities. Chandrayaan-2 was launched on July 22, 2019.

"Chandrayaan-3" is India's third lunar exploration mission, launched by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). It builds upon the success of the first Chandrayaan mission and is designed to further advance India's lunar exploration capabilities. Chandrayaan-3 was landed on Aug 23, 2023.
